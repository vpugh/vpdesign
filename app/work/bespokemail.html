BespokeMail Interface

The creation of a graphical user interface (GUI) to facilitate easier email development and deployment.

UX/UI, Web Design, Web Development, Email Design, Email Development

The Brief

After creating a code-based solution to managing different templates of email and incorporating large amounts of data into them I realized this isn't the most user-friendly. I decided to make a system to be used by non-coders that had the same level of use. It would rely on a base of code and then is would self reliant on the data and the person entering that in.

The Approach

My first idea was to create a computer-based app. This would be powered by Electron a software that powers many popular app, one being Spotify. I enjoy using Spotify and thought the layout they used might be conducive to the project. The setup with the dashboard on the left and the editor/main content on the right seemed ideal for visual editing. These are the steps I took in the first iteration.

Trying to imitate Spotify and VS Code but with a lighter palette

A good start but I was having trouble developing with Electron and didn't like the look of the layout. I'm not sure I was ever a 100% sold on the tabbing system and having all the settings in little drop-down menus all over the window. I tried to emulate VS Code too much. I didn't account for my content in the window. It started getting cluttered and jumbled.

Iteration #2

After abandoning my Spotify inspired version, I searched for a better layout. Reverting to something cleaner and more user-friendly I settled on a left side full of inputs and drop-down menus. With the right side where all the visual elements would happen. The visual side is big enough to accomodate the "suggested size" of email (600px wide).

Right now its more a visual setup preparing for the behind the scenes functionality. I was more concerned with creating a smooth experience for editing and managing emails. I grouped content together and even now think I could combine some forms together and separate them from other sections to streamline the forms.

See the Pen Vue - Email Workflow GUI - v3 by Tori (@Vpugh) on CodePen.
